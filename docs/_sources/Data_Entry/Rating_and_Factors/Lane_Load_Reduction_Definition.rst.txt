<P style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0">&nbsp;</P>
<H2><IMG src="..\..\image\ebd_Ebd70.gif" border="0" height="39" width="320" alt="image\ebd_Ebd70.gif"></H2>
<H2>Description</H2>
<P style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0">&nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin-left:36.00pt;">These factors are applied directly to the <A href="..\..\Load_Generation\Load_Combination_Theory.html#Truck_Path_Envelope_TPE">TPEs</A> when building the composite load combination force envelopes.  Refer to the AASHTO live load specification for “Reduction in Load Intensity” for the values to use.</P>
<P style="margin-left:36.00pt;">&nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin-left:36.00pt;">From the 1996 “Standard Specifications for Highway Bridges” Sixteenth Edition section 3.12.1</P>
<P style="margin-left:36.00pt;text-indent:36.00pt;">For 1&amp;2 lanes loaded=1.0</P>
<P style="margin-left:36.00pt;text-indent:36.00pt;">3 lanes loaded=0.9</P>
<P style="margin-left:36.00pt;text-indent:36.00pt;">4 or more lanes loaded=0.75</P>
<H2>Parameters</H2>
<P style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0">&nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin-left:72.00pt;text-indent:-36.00pt;"><span style="font-weight:bold; ">1 Lane&nbsp;</span>Factor that is applied to the TPE if only one lane is loaded.&nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin-left:72.00pt;text-indent:-36.00pt;"><span style="font-weight:bold; ">2 Lane&nbsp;</span>Factor that is applied to the TPE if two lanes are loaded.&nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin-left:72.00pt;text-indent:-36.00pt;"><span style="font-weight:bold; ">3 Lane&nbsp;</span>Factor that is applied to the TPE if three lanes are loaded.&nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin-left:72.00pt;text-indent:-36.00pt;"><span style="font-weight:bold; ">4 Lane&nbsp;</span>Factor that is applied to the TPE if four or more lanes are loaded.</P>
